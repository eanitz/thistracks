Creating a Communication Plan to Stay Connected During Disasters

In the realm of prepping, homesteading, and survivalism, maintaining communication during disasters is paramount. This article aims to guide you through the process of creating an effective communication plan that ensures you stay connected with your loved ones and community in times of crisis.

Identify Your Communication Needs

Before delving into the technical aspects, it's crucial to identify what your communication needs are. This includes determining who needs to stay connected (family, friends, or community members), what information needs to be shared (location, status, supplies, etc.), and under what circumstances you would need to communicate (during an evacuation, sheltering in place, or while on the move).

Choose Appropriate Communication Methods

Different communication methods work better for different situations. Here are some options:

  • Landline Phones: These are reliable during power outages if you have a corded phone and a traditional landline service. However, they become useless in the event of widespread infrastructure damage.
  • Cell Phones: Smartphones are versatile tools that can access numerous communication methods like text messaging, phone calls, social media, and satellite-based services. But cell towers may be down or overloaded during disasters.
  • Two-Way Radios: These devices offer a more reliable means of communication in the event of widespread power outages. They can operate on multiple frequencies to avoid interference and have longer battery lives than smartphones.
  • Ham Radio: Amateur radios can be an excellent choice for long-range communication, especially during disasters when other means might fail. Licensing requirements apply.
  • Satellite Phones: These devices can provide global coverage through satellite connections, making them ideal for remote locations or international emergencies.

Establish a Communication Strategy

A good communication strategy involves redundancy and prioritization. Ensure you have multiple methods of communication available, and have contingency plans for when one method fails. Create a Communication Plan

Your communication plan should outline the following:

  • Contact Information: List all phone numbers, email addresses, and physical addresses for your contacts.
  • Communication Methods: Specify which methods you will use in various scenarios (local emergencies, regional disasters, or wide-scale crises).
  • Check-in Procedures: Establish regular check-ins to confirm everyone's safety and location.
  • Emergency Meetup Points: Designate safe locations where you can regroup in case of evacuation or localized disasters.

Practice Your Plan

Regularly practice your communication plan to ensure everyone is familiar with the procedures and can execute them efficiently. This will help reduce panic and confusion during a real disaster.

Maintain and Update Your Plan

Ensure all communication equipment is well-maintained, batteries are charged, and devices are updated regularly. Also, update your plan as needed to account for changes in your living situation or family structure.
